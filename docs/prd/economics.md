## Topic 1: Microeconomic Analysis

---

### Elasticity Formulas

**1. Price Elasticity of Demand (Point Method)**
$$E_d = \frac{\% \text{ Change in Quantity Demanded of Product}}{\% \text{ Change in Price of Product}}$$

**2. Price Elasticity of Demand (Arc / Midpoint Method)**
$$E_d = \frac{\dfrac{|\text{Change in Quantity Demanded}|}{\text{Sum of Quantity Demanded} \div 2}}{\dfrac{|\text{Change in Price}|}{\text{Sum of Prices} \div 2}}$$

> The **midpoint method** uses averages as the base to give a consistent elasticity value regardless of direction of price change.

---

### Elasticity & Total Revenue

| Elasticity of Demand | Change in Price | Effect on Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Elastic ($E_d > 1$) | Increase | Decrease |
| Elastic ($E_d > 1$) | Decrease | Increase |
| Inelastic ($E_d < 1$) | Increase | Increase |
| Inelastic ($E_d < 1$) | Decrease | Decrease |
| Unitary Elastic ($E_d = 1$) | Increase | No Change |
| Unitary Elastic ($E_d = 1$) | Decrease | No Change |
| Perfectly Inelastic ($E_d = 0$) | Increase | Increase |
| Perfectly Inelastic ($E_d = 0$) | Decrease | Decrease |

### Cost Concepts

**3. Sunk Cost**
$$\text{Sunk Cost} = \text{Historical Cost}$$

> Sunk costs are **irrelevant to future decisions** — they cannot be recovered regardless of the action taken.

**4. Fixed Costs** — costs that do not vary with output level *(no explicit formula stored)*

**5. Variable Costs** — costs that change with output level *(no explicit formula stored)*

**6. Average Cost** — total cost per unit of output *(no explicit formula stored)*

**7. Marginal Cost** — additional cost of producing one more unit *(no explicit formula stored)*

**8. Opportunity Cost** — value of the best alternative forgone *(no explicit formula stored)*

## Topic 2: Macroeconomic Analysis

**1. Consumer Price Index (CPI)**
$$\text{CPI} = \frac{\text{Updated Cost}}{\text{Base Cost}} \times 100$$

**2. Inflation Rate**
$$\text{Inflation Rate} = \frac{\text{Current CPI}}{\text{Previous CPI}}$$
